Building the Coin Box Enclosure

Building the Box

Step 1. Cut the Wood

Cut two slightly larger pieces (top & bottom) and four slightly smaller pieces (the sides). Exact dimensions depend on your desired box size.

Step 2. Glue the Pieces Together

Glue the six panels to form a box. Ensure all edges are flush and corners perfectly square.

Step 3. Secure the Bottom

Reinforce the bottom with a handful of countersunk screws or nails. Glue alone can work, but fasteners add durability.

Step 4. Lid Slit

Cut a coin slot through the lid. Ours is X mm × Y mm—wide enough for the biggest coin you expect.

Step 5. Hole for DC Jack

Drill a hole on one side for the DC power jack. We used a PCB‑mount jack glued in place and soldered a JST lead for easy disconnection.

Step 6. Paint the Base Coat

Apply a white acrylic base coat. It makes the final colours pop and seals the wood.

Step 7. Draw the Pixel Grid

Lightly sketch a 16 × 16 pixel grid on every visible face. This guides the Mario Question‑Block artwork.

Step 8. Paint the Pixel Art

Fill the grid with bright colours to create the iconic Question Block.

Step 9. Magnets for the Lid

Drill shallow holes in each corner of the box and lid, then epoxy small magnets inside. The lid now snaps on and off for maintenance.
